Cowboy Cookies

Description

A delicious blend of butter, sugar, chocolate chips, pecans, and oats that will satisfy any cookie lover.

Recipe makes 4 to 5 dozen cookies.

NOTE: Crushed pecans can be left out or substituted for crushed nut of choice. Any chocolate chips work fine, I use dark chocolate chips but I find most people prefer semisweet.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 sticks of unsalted butter (softened)
  • 2/3 cup white sugar
  • 2/3 cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup crushed pecan
  • 1 cup chocolate chips of choice

Instructions

NOTE: Having 2 baking sheets speeds up cooking process a lot. I personally use 3 because my wire rack is very small. The third one stays on the counter as the final cooling area before cookies are stored.

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees and grease baking sheets.
  2. Sift flour, salt, baking soda, and baking powder in a bowl. (if you do not have a sifter, vigorously whisk ingredients together in bowl for several minutes)
  3. Mix softened butter in a large bowl with electric mixer until it looks fluffy.
  4. Slowly add white sugar and brown sugar to butter and continue mixing until well mixed and fluffy.
  5. Add one egg at a time and continue mixing.
  6. Add vanilla and continue mixing.
  7. Slowly add flour mixture and continue mixing until well mixed and cookie dough has formed.
  8. Fold in chocolate chips, oats, and crushed pecans.
  9. Drop slightly smaller than golf ball sized scoops of dough onto greased baking sheet.
  10. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes.
  11. Prepare next batch to bake.
  12. While next batch is baking allow to cool for at least 5 minutes on baking sheet before transferring cookies to wherever you have prepared to completely cool.
